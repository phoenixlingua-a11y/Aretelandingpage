Founder's note

Why I started Areté.

We instrument everything. Steps, sleep, heart rate, revenue, every variable except the one that quietly decides all the others: the state of the mind at the moment it makes the call. The most consequential decisions a person makes go completely unmeasured. That state gets treated as sacred, unknowable. I never believed that.

I studied philosophy and physics at LMU Munich, two fields that ask the same question from opposite ends: what makes a decision sound? Physics taught me that anything real leaves a signal; you just have to build the instrument patient enough to read it. Philosophy taught me what is actually at stake when a mind decides under load: reasoning, bias, integrity.

Areté is the instrument I went looking for and couldn't find: a continuous, honest read of your cognitive integrity, delivered before the call, not after. Not a personality test. A check engine light for decision-making. I'm building it because the gap it closes is the one I've spent my whole life staring into.
